Project Description

The COVID-19 data visualization project will present a comprehensive overview of key statistics, trends, and patterns related to certain health factors. It will utilize various visualization techniques to effectively communicate complex data in an accessible and understandable format.

Key Features:

  • Comparative Analysis: Enable users to compare COVID-19 statistics between countries or regions to identify disparities and trends.
  • Predictive Modeling: Integrate predictive models to forecast future COVID-19 cases in relation to certain health factors and assess the potential impact of interventions.
  • Vulnerable Populations Analysis: Identify and visualize populations at higher risk of severe COVID-19 outcomes, such as older adults and individuals with pre-existing conditions.
  • Public Policy Evaluation: Assess the effectiveness of COVID-19 mitigation measures, such as lockdowns, mask mandates, and social distancing policies, through visualization of infection rates and policy implementation timelines.

Target Audience

The target audience for the COVID-19 data visualization project includes policymakers, healthcare professionals, researchers, students, and the general public interested in understanding the dynamics of the pandemic and making informed decisions.

Time Line

Week 1: Data collection and preprocessing. Designing the layout and functionality of the visualization.

Week 2: Implementation of interactive features and visualization techniques. Testing for usability and functionality.

Week 3: Finalizing visual design elements. Conducting user testing and gathering feedback for refinement.

Week 4: Adding explanatory text and documentation. Presenting the finalized project to the class.

Deliverables

  • Interactive data visualization platform showcasing COVID-19 statistics and trends.
  • Documentation outlining data sources, methodologies, and design decisions.
  • Source code repository for the project, accessible for further development and collaboration.
